Hi! I'm a third-year undergraduate software engineering student at the University of Waterloo. I currently do research on open-ended learning for embodied foundation models as a visiting undergraduate researcher at Caltech advised by Guanzhi Wang, Jim Fan, and Anima Anandkumar.

I'm interested in a variety of topics around computer science and machine learning. My current research interests are mainly related to improving how we train machine learning models through better optimization algorithms and taking a principled approach to design and train better foundation models.

Previously, I was a student researcher at Google Brain on the algorithmic efficiency team (machine learning optimizers and training efficiency) hosted by Zachary Nado and Priya Kasimbeg. Before that, I spent two amazing internships at Cohere with Stephen Guo and Bharat Venkitesh where I wrote TF 1.X, TF 2.0, JAX, and CUDA to efficiently scale both the training and inference of O(50B) parameter large language models to exaflop-scale TPU and GPU clusters. In my free time I enjoy travelling and exploring new cities, eating, and playing Valorant (on hold as I only own a mac rn). Undergrad AI research advice.

Benchmarking Neural Network Training Algorithms
George E. Dahl, Frank Schneider, Zachary Nado, Naman Agarwal, Chandramouli Shama Sastry, Philipp Henning Sourabh Medapati, Runa Eschenhagen, Priya Kasimbeg, Daniel Suo, Juhan Bae, Justin Gilmer, Abel L. Peirson, Bilal Khan, Rohan Anil, Mike Rabbat, Shankar Krishnan, Daniel Snider, Ehsan Amid, Kongtao Chen, Chris J. Maddison, Rakshith Vasudev, Michal Badura, Ankush Garg, Peter Mattson

We introduce the AlgoPerf: Training Algorithms benchmark ‚Äî a competitive, time-to-result benchmark on multiple workloads running on fixed hardware for systematically comparing training algorithms. We construct baselines by defining search spaces for eight popular optimizers (AdamW, NadamW, Heavy Ball, Nesterov, LAMB, Adafactor, SAM(w. Adam), and Distributed Shampoo) that includes both popular optimizers that represent current practice and methods that have received attention in the recent literature and compare their performance on our benchmark.
